## Bell Mountain Bike Helmet

**Safety flag — not listed.**

Under Australian consumer safety rules, used bicycle helmets (including mountain bike helmets) must comply with AS/NZS 2063. The standard sets a service life (typically 3–5 years from manufacture, or sooner after any impact), and helmets that have been worn cannot be verified as impact-free by a buyer. Facebook Marketplace and Gumtree AU both prohibit the sale of used helmets that can no longer be confirmed safe, and reselling a helmet that later fails in a crash creates real liability.

**Next best action:**
- Check the manufacture date on the inside liner. If it's within 3–5 years and the helmet has never taken an impact, some local cycling clubs and community Facebook groups will accept donated helmets for low-income riders — contact them directly.
- Otherwise, dispose of it by cutting the straps (so it can't be reused) and placing it in general waste. It is not recyclable kerbside.
- If it's a recent model in truly as-new condition and you're confident it's never been dropped or crashed in, you could sell it privately to someone you know and can vouch for its history — but a public anonymous listing is not recommended.

## Britax Child Car Seat

**Safety flag — not listed.**

Child car seats sold in Australia must comply with AS/NZS 1754. The standard requires that seats not be used beyond their expiry date (stamped on the seat — typically 8–10 years from manufacture), and critically, that seats not be resold or reused after any vehicle collision — even a minor one. A buyer on Marketplace or Gumtree has no way to verify the seat's crash history, and a compromised seat can fail silently in a real accident.

Facebook Marketplace and Gumtree AU both prohibit the resale of used child car seats for this reason.

**Next best action:**
- Check the expiry date stamped on the seat. If it hasn't expired and you are certain it has never been in a collision:
  - **Donate to a trusted family member or friend** where you can personally vouch for the history — this is the safest form of reuse.
  - **Baby equipment charities** such as Anglicare, the Smith Family, or local Buy Nothing groups sometimes accept seats with a clean history and valid expiry — call first to confirm.
- If it is expired, or you're unsure of its collision history: the ACCC recommends disposing of it in a way that prevents reuse — cut the harness straps, write "DO NOT USE — EXPIRED" on the shell in marker, then place in general waste. It is not kerbside-recyclable.

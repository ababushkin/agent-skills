## Safety flag — damaged cord

Not okay to sell as-is, unfortunately.

A visible nick in the cord insulation near the plug is an electrical safety hazard. Under Australian Consumer Law and the Australian Electrical Equipment Safety System (EESS), selling secondhand electrical goods with damaged cords is either prohibited or requires the item to be clearly advertised as **"for parts / not working / untested"** — and many platforms (Facebook Marketplace, Gumtree) will remove listings that show visible cord damage if flagged, because the seller is potentially liable if a buyer is injured.

This isn't a technicality: exposed insulation near the plug is a genuine shock and fire risk. It's not in the same category as cosmetic scratches.

---

## Your two real options

### Option 1 — Get the cord repaired first (recommended)

A licensed electrician can replace a juicer power cord for roughly **$40–$80 all-in**, sometimes less at an appliance repair shop. Once repaired, a working Breville juicer in good condition lists for:

- Comp range seen: $40–$125 on Gumtree AU and Facebook Marketplace Melbourne (mid-range models like the BJE430/BJE510 in good condition sit around $50–$90; entry-level units around $25–$50)
- A repaired, working unit in good condition would realistically list at **$55–$75 ono**
- Repair cost of ~$60 is worth it if the unit is a mid-range or higher model — you'd recover the cost and likely net $0–$30 profit, or simply get a fair price rather than nothing

### Option 2 — Sell as "for parts / not working"

You can list it honestly as damaged/for parts. This is legal, but the return is low:

- Parts-only Breville juicers (or units sold "as is — cord issue") typically fetch **$10–$20** on Gumtree AU, sometimes nothing
- The listing must clearly state: *"Power cord has damaged insulation near plug — sold as-is for parts or repair only. Not tested."* Omitting this is misrepresentation.
- At that price, it may not be worth the effort of listing — a garage-sale "as-is" table or a local Buy Nothing group might be the easier route

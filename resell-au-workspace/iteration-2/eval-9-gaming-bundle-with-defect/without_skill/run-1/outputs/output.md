### Pricing Assessment for This Bundle

**What you have:**
- Switch OLED console (great condition, perfect screen) ✅ — strong asset
- Dock ✅
- 2 x Joy-Con pairs (one left Joy-Con has noticeable drift) ⚠️ — disclosure required, mild price impact
- 3 games in original boxes (Zelda BotW, Mario Kart 8 Deluxe, Animal Crossing NH) ✅ — high-value, popular titles

**The drift factor:** Joy-Con drift is common and known. Many buyers are aware they can recalibrate in settings or get Nintendo to repair it (Nintendo has a free/subsidised repair program). Being upfront about it builds trust and protects you from disputes. It will reduce the price slightly but not dramatically — perhaps $20–$30 off vs. a perfect set.

**Recommended asking price: $420–$450**

- A clean Switch OLED with accessories alone sells for $280–$350 privately

- This puts a fair ask at **$420–$450**, with room to negotiate to around **$390–$400**
- List at $450, be willing to accept $400 from a genuine buyer

### Tips for Selling

1. **Lead with the positives** — perfect OLED screen and three great titles are strong selling points. List those first.
2. **Be upfront about the drift** — mention it clearly rather than burying it. Buyers who find out post-sale create disputes and bad reviews. Honest disclosure builds trust and attracts serious buyers.
3. **Show, don't just tell** — include photos of the console screen on, the game cases open, and all controllers. Take a short video showing the drift so there's no ambiguity.
4. **Know your floor** — $390–$400 is a reasonable bottom. Below that, selling the games separately (Mario Kart and BotW in particular) may net you more.
5. **Selling separately as an option:** If no bundle buyer appears within 2–3 weeks, consider splitting:
   - Console + dock + all Joy-Cons: $280–$320 (disclosing drift)
   - Mario Kart 8 Deluxe: $50–$60
   - Zelda BotW: $30–$40
   - Animal Crossing: $20–$25
   - Potential separate total: **$380–$445**

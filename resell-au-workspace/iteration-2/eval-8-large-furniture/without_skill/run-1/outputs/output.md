**Key observations:**
- Leather couches in genuinely good condition (no stains, no tears) sell in the **$200–$400 range** in Melbourne
- Armrest wear is common and buyers expect it — it does push you toward the lower end of the range if visible in photos
- Age of 6 years is a moderate negative factor
- Being in the eastern suburbs (Box Hill) is a reasonable location for buyers across Metro Melbourne
- The "buyer must organise a ute/van" requirement reduces the buyer pool but is normal for large couches

**Recommended asking price: $250**

This is realistic given the armrest wear and age. Start at $280–$300 if you want a small buffer for negotiation, or list at $250 firm if you want faster movement. Pricing below $200 is unnecessary unless it's sat unsold for 3+ weeks.

## Tips for a Fast, Smooth Sale

1. **Photos matter most.** Take 6–8 clear photos in good natural light: full front view, each end, top of armrests (show the wear honestly), cushion tops, back of couch. Hiding the armrest wear will lead to wasted inspections and lowball offers on the day.

2. **Post on both Facebook Marketplace and Gumtree.** Marketplace gets more views but Gumtree still has an active Melbourne audience for furniture.

3. **Mention dimensions in your first reply.** Many buyers will ask — have the measurements (length, depth, height) ready.

4. **Be specific about the ute/van requirement upfront.** This saves time-wasters who show up with a hatchback.

5. **Avoid holds.** Leather couches attract browsers — only hold for someone who has confirmed a specific pickup time, and don't hold longer than 48 hours.

6. **If it hasn't sold in 2–3 weeks,** drop to $200 or add "open to offers."

### Key Pricing Factors for Your Camera

**Strengths pushing price up:**
- Fully working — shutter fires across all speeds, meter reads accurately (recently tested)
- Original 50mm f/2 SMC Pentax lens (this is the desirable version; f/1.7 is rarer but f/2 is the classic pairing)
- Original neck strap included
- UV filter on lens (minor but noted)
- Brassing on corners only — cosmetically honest and expected for a vintage piece; not a red flag for buyers

**Factors to be aware of:**
- No case (minor negative; buyers often don't care)
- Brassing means it shows its age — price accordingly vs. "near mint" examples

### Recommended Asking Price

**$280–$320 AUD** is the sweet spot for a private seller with this setup.

- Retail shops (Australian Analog, Ringwood Camera House) price serviced cameras with warranty at $495+. Your private listing can undercut them and still fetch a fair price.
- Gumtree comps without extras (zoom lens, case, warranty) cluster around $350–$390. Your camera lacks a case but has a tested, accurate meter and original strap.
- At $280–$300 you'll sell quickly. At $320 you may take a week or two but still have room to negotiate.
- Mark it as negotiable. Film camera buyers almost always ask, and having headroom to drop $20–$30 closes deals.

**Best platform choice:**
- Facebook Marketplace (local pickup, no fees, fastest)
- Gumtree (good for non-metro reach)
- eBay Australia if you're happy to post (broadens buyer pool, add $10–$15 for postage)

## Recommended Price

**$65–$80** is the realistic range for a quick sale.

- The cracked/taped headband is a visible cosmetic defect that immediately signals risk to buyers
- Sound and ANC being fully functional is a genuine selling point that saves it from parts-only pricing
- Includes the hard case and USB-C cable (adds perceived value vs. bare listings)
- Pricing at $75 is competitive without underselling — it leaves a little room to negotiate down to $65 if a buyer haggles

If you want to test the market before dropping low, open at **$90** and be prepared to drop. But $75 is the "sell it this week" price.

## Listing Tips

1. **Lead with the defect in the title.** Buyers who are bothered by it will self-select out and you won't waste time. Buyers who know this model's headband issue (it's widely discussed) will respect the honesty.

2. **Use the phrase "known issue with this model."** It signals you've researched it, not hiding anything, and reassures buyers the sound isn't affected.

3. **Photos to include:**
   - The taped headband (close up — do not hide this)
   - Both ear cups with pads on
   - The hard case open with everything inside
   - Bluetooth pairing screen on a phone (proves it connects)

4. **Respond quickly.** At $75 you'll get interest, but also low-ballers. Set a floor of $60 in your head and hold it — the case and cable justify at least that.

5. **Note the headband repair cost in messaging if asked.** A replacement headband for XM3 runs ~$15–$25 on eBay AU, which a buyer can use to justify the purchase to themselves.
